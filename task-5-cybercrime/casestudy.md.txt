### Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source. This usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites.

In a typical phishing attack, a student might receive an email that appears to be from a bank or a popular service asking them to verify their account details. The email contains a link that redirects to a fake website that looks identical to the real one. When the user enters their login details, the attacker captures this information and uses it for unauthorized access.

Students and young users are often targeted because they may not recognize such scams easily. The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal information.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking links and sharing sensitive information online.
